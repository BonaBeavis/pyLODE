Description

The format of an item within a collection describes its physical presence at the highest level.

History Note: This vocabulary has been developed by referencing publicly available information from select libraries and archives about their FORMAT cataloguing terms.

Disccp

IRI http://linked.data.gov.au/def/collection-item-format/disc
Definition A sound recording on a thin, flat circular object, usually made of shellac, vinyl, or various laminates. The signal may be either analogue or digital, and recorded/played using acoustical, electrical, magnetic or optical technology.
Source https://www.iasa-web.org/cataloguing-rules/appendix-d-glossary

Diskcp

IRI http://linked.data.gov.au/def/collection-item-format/disk
Definition A round magnetic device for storing information and programmes accessible by computer; may be either a rigid platter (hard disk) or a sheet of flexible plastic (floppy disk or diskette). The disk base is coated with a magnetizable material on which data can be recorded or stored along concentric tracks as small magnetic areas forming patterns of binary digits or bits. Information is written onto the disk, and read from it in a disk drive, by read/write heads mounted on arms which move rapidly across the disk.
Source https://www.iasa-web.org/cataloguing-rules/appendix-d-glossary
Broader Concepts cif:digitalcp
